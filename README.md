# levigoodman23-Elden-Ring
Tis a website about the greatest game in the world Elden Ring.
Long ago, a powerful force called the Elden Ring controlled the laws of the world. It was tied to a godlike being called the Greater Will, who chose Queen Marika the Eternal to rule the world known as the Lands Between.
Everything was stable — until the Elden Ring was shattered.
No one knows exactly why at first, but after it broke:
Marika disappeared.
Her children (demigods) took pieces of the Ring called Great Runes.
They went to war with each other.
The world fell into chaos and decay.
You play as a Tarnished — someone who was once exiled from the Lands Between. After the world falls apart, you are called back.
Your goal is simple:
Defeat the demigods
Collect their Great Runes
Repair the Elden Ring
Become the new Elden Lord
As you explore, you learn:
The gods may not be as good as they seem.
The Greater Will might be controlling everything.
Queen Marika may have shattered the Ring on purpose.
In the end, you choose how the world will be remade — restore the old order, change it, or destroy it completely.
It’s the story of a broken world where you rise from exile to decide the fate of gods, order, and reality itself.
At the end of the game, after defeating the final boss, you choose how to remake the world. There are 6 main endings, but they fall into 3 basic categories:
Age of Fracture (Default Ending)
You repair the Elden Ring and become Elden Lord.
The world continues mostly the same.
The Golden Order remains.
You take the throne.
Age of the Stars
Linked to Ranni the Witch.
Ranni replaces the Greater Will.
The world enters a cold, distant “Age of the Stars.”
Gods lose direct control.
Humanity gains more freedom.
Many players consider this the “good” ending.
Lord of the Frenzied Flame
No traditional NPC ending — tied to the Three Fingers.
You burn the entire world.
Chaos replaces order.
Everything is destroyed in madness and fire.
This is the “destroy everything” ending.
